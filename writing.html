Bridging language barriers between hosts and guests on Airbnb

Having lived in an English-speaking country for almost 10 years, my family has become more and more comfortable about sharing homes with non-Mandarin speakers. However, not every host will speak a second language, and not every traveler will have a working knowledge of all the languages native to their destinations. What kind of problems may arise when a host and a guest attempt to connect through a language barrier? Third Tone Sandhi in Mandarin

This paper examines third tone sandhi in Mandarin Chinese, a Sinitic language predominantly used in mainland China. My analysis examines three possible hypotheses for third tone sandhi, between that of Cheng, Lu, and a hypothesis on semantic influence in sandhi application. We arrive at the conclusion that although Cheng’s iterative rule is correct, the rule must be applied in order of the phrases’ syntactic hierarchy. Our final conclusion is affirmed by correct derivations of trisyllabic phrases for all possible syntactic structures, and complemented by a possible explanation for the pronunciation of trisyllabic third tone name clusters.

Language processing in bimodal bilingual children during their critical periods: The effects of simultaneous bimodal input on infant word segmentation

Sensorineural hearing loss is the most frequently-occurring birth defect globally, with more than 90% of deaf newborns being born into a family that does not utilize sign language. The cochlear implant (CI), an electronic ear prosthesis which directly stimulates the auditory nerve and partially restores access to speech perception, has become the treatment of choice for the majority of such infants (Giezen, 2011). The influx of implantation in children has resulted in growing cases of bimodal bilingualism, the simultaneous acquisition of a visual sign language and an auditory spoken language. This section examines English literature and publications surrounding the investigation of bimodal bilingualism and its effects on child language development.

A stranger asked me to delete her Facebook and Messenger

It’s understandable that the scary thought of user attrition can lead to acts of desperation, but it is nevertheless important to commit to the healthy interactions we so value in our lives. Extending the (totally impersonal) relationship metaphor, we should keep a user through positive influence, trust-building, and patiently improving from our own faults. If a break from our product is needed, we can, and should do little but wave a tearful goodbye and promise that we will improve in the future — and meet them again, if it’s meant to be.
